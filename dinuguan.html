Dinuguan

Main Course 1 hour Serves 6 Moderate

4.5 (112 reviews)

Rich, Bold, and Comforting

Dinuguan is a unique Filipino savory stew made from pork, vinegar, garlic, chili, and fresh pig's blood. Despite its dramatic dark color, it’s a beloved dish known for its bold, savory-sour flavor and is often served with **puto** (steamed rice cakes).

Whether you're trying it for the first time or recreating a family recipe, this dish is packed with authentic Filipino character and soul.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pork belly or shoulder, cut into small cubes
  • 1/2 lb pork liver (optional), chopped
  • 1 cup fresh pig’s blood
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1-2 long green chili peppers
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup water or pork broth

Instructions

  1. In a pot, heat oil and sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.
  2. Add pork cubes and cook until lightly browned. If using liver, add it once pork is half cooked.
  3. Pour in vinegar and let it boil without stirring to cook off the acidity.
  4. Pour in water or broth, season with salt and pepper, and let simmer for 25–30 minutes or until pork is tender.
  5. Lower the heat and slowly pour in the pig's blood while stirring continuously to avoid curdling.
  6. Add chili peppers and continue to simmer for another 10–15 minutes until thick and rich.
  7. Adjust seasoning if needed and serve hot with rice or puto.

Tips & Variations

  • Fresh Blood Only: Ensure the blood is fresh and strained to remove clots.
  • Texture: Add intestines or pork skin for a more authentic texture if desired.
  • No Liver? Omit it if you prefer a milder flavor.
  • Serve With: Best paired with steamed white rice or puto for balance.
  • Storage: Keeps well in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat gently to preserve texture.
