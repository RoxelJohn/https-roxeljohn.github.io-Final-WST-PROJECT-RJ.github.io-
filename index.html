Why Filipino Food?

Filipino cuisine is a delightful fusion of flavors influenced by Malay, Spanish, Chinese, and American cultures, creating a unique taste experience you won't find anywhere else.

Signature Dish

Chicken Adobo

The Philippines' national dish features meat marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices, then simmered to perfection.

Tart & Tangy

Sinigang

A sour soup made with tamarind as the base flavor, combined with various vegetables and your choice of meat or seafood.

Grand Celebration

Lechon

A whole roasted pig with crisp, golden-brown skin and tender, flavorful meat, often the centerpiece of Filipino celebrations.

Hearty Classic

Kare-Kare

A rich oxtail stew with vegetables in a thick peanut sauce, traditionally served with bagoong (shrimp paste).

Summer Favorite

Halo-Halo

A colorful dessert with shaved ice, evaporated milk, and various ingredients like sweet beans, fruits, and leche flan.

Crunchy Bites

Lumpia

Filipino spring rolls with various fillings, either fresh (lumpiang sariwa) or fried (lumpiang prito).
