Chicken Adobo

Main Course 1 hour Serves 4 Medium

4.5 (120 reviews)

The Philippines' National Dish

Chicken Adobo is considered the national dish of the Philippines for good reason. This savory, slightly tangy, and garlicky dish is a perfect representation of Filipino cuisine - simple ingredients transformed into something extraordinary through careful cooking.

The magic of adobo lies in its perfect balance of salty, sour, and savory flavors. The chicken is marinated and simmered in a mixture of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices, resulting in tender meat with a deliciously flavorful sauce.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken (cut into serving pieces)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup white vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 head garlic (crushed)
  • 1 teaspoon whole peppercorns
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine chicken, soy sauce, and garlic. Marinate for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  2. Heat oil in a pot over medium heat. Remove chicken from marinade (reserve marinade) and brown on all sides.
  3. Add the reserved marinade, water, vinegar, peppercorns, and bay leaves. Bring to a boil then lower heat to simmer.
  4. Cover and simmer for 30 minutes or until chicken is tender. Stir occasionally.
  5. Remove the cover and add brown sugar if using. Continue simmering until sauce reduces and thickens (about 10-15 minutes).
  6. Season with salt if needed. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Tips & Variations

  • For a more authentic taste, use coconut vinegar instead of white vinegar.
  • Some versions add coconut milk (Adobo sa Gata) for a richer, creamier sauce.
  • You can use pork instead of chicken or a combination of both.
  • Leftover adobo tastes even better the next day as the flavors continue to develop.
  • For a crispy version, fry the cooked adobo pieces in hot oil until browned.
