Beef Mechado

Main Course 1.5 hours Serves 6 Moderate

4.0 (89 reviews)

Hearty and Rich with Tomato Goodness

Beef Mechado is a traditional Filipino stew with Spanish influence. It’s known for its slow-cooked beef chunks simmered in a tomato-based sauce, with vegetables and spices creating a deep, comforting flavor perfect for family meals.

The dish gets its name from the Spanish word *mecha* (wick), referencing how early versions were larded with pork fat, like a candle wick through meat. Today, it’s a beloved staple across Filipino homes.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef chuck or brisket, cut into cubes
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup calamansi or lemon juice
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups tomato sauce
  • 2 cups beef broth or water
  • 2 medium potatoes, quartered
  • 1 large carrot, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Bay leaves (optional)

Instructions

  1. Marinate beef in soy sauce and calamansi juice for at least 1 hour. Drain and set aside marinade.
  2. In a large pot, heat oil and sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.
  3. Add marinated beef and cook until browned on all sides.
  4. Pour in tomato sauce and reserved marinade. Mix well.
  5. Add beef broth, bay leaves, and bring to a boil. Lower heat, cover, and simmer for 1 to 1.5 hours or until beef is tender.
  6. Add potatoes and carrots. Cook until tender, about 10–15 minutes.
  7. Add bell peppers, season with salt and pepper, and simmer for another 5 minutes.
  8. Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy!

Tips & Variations

  • Thicker Sauce: Simmer uncovered for the last 10 minutes to reduce sauce if desired.
  • Classic Mecha: Insert pork fat strips into beef for a more traditional version.
  • Sour Element: Add a splash of vinegar if you prefer a tangier profile.
  • Alternative Protein: Try pork or chicken for variety.
  • Slow Cooker: Great for slow cooker adaptation – just brown beef first.
