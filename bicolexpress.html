Bicol Express

Pork Dish 50 minutes Serves 4–5 Spicy

4.0 (87 reviews)

A Spicy Bicolano Favorite

Bicol Express is a popular Filipino dish known for its rich coconut milk base and fiery chili heat. Originating from the Bicol region, where coconut and chili peppers are abundant, this dish combines pork, shrimp paste, and spices in a creamy, spicy stew that pairs perfectly with hot steamed rice.

If you love bold flavors and creamy spice, this one's for you!

Ingredients

  • 1/2 kilo pork belly, sliced into small cubes
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece ginger, sliced
  • 3 tablespoons shrimp paste (bagoong alamang)
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 cup coconut cream (kakang gata)
  • 6–8 green and red chili peppers, sliced diagonally
  • 2–3 siling labuyo (optional, for extra heat)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Sauté garlic, onion, and ginger until aromatic.
  2. Add pork belly and cook until lightly browned and fat is rendered.
  3. Stir in shrimp paste and cook for 2–3 minutes.
  4. Pour in coconut milk. Bring to a boil then reduce heat to simmer for 20–25 minutes.
  5. Add chili peppers and siling labuyo (if using). Stir gently.
  6. Pour in the coconut cream and continue simmering until sauce thickens and pork is tender.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot with rice.

Tips & Variations

  • Vegetarian Option: Substitute pork with tofu or mushrooms and skip the shrimp paste.
  • Milder Version: Use fewer chilies and remove the seeds to tone down the heat.
  • More Sauce? Add extra coconut milk for a saucier dish.
  • Extra Umami: Add a few pieces of shrimp or dried fish for depth of flavor.

Nutrition Information
