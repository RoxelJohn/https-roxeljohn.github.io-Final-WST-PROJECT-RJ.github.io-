Pork Menudo

Main Course 1 hour 30 mins Serves 6 Medium

4.6 (85 reviews)

The Festive Filipino Stew

Pork Menudo is a beloved Filipino stew that graces tables during special occasions and family gatherings. This colorful dish combines tender pork, liver, and vegetables in a rich tomato-based sauce with a perfect balance of savory and slightly sweet flavors.

What makes Filipino menudo unique is the addition of liver (unlike its Mexican counterpart) and the vibrant mix of potatoes, carrots, bell peppers, and raisins that create a symphony of textures and flavors in every bite.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork shoulder, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1/2 lb pork liver, cut into small cubes
  • 3 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 carrots, peeled and cubed
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 2 cups pork or chicken broth
  • 3 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Saut√© garlic and onions until fragrant.
  2. Add pork cubes and cook until lightly browned on all sides (about 5-7 minutes).
  3. Add liver and cook for 2 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Pour in soy sauce and vinegar. Let simmer for 3 minutes without stirring.
  5. Add tomato sauce and broth. Bring to a boil then lower heat to simmer.
  6. Cover and simmer for 30 minutes or until pork is tender.
  7. Add potatoes and carrots. Simmer for another 10 minutes.
  8. Add bell peppers, raisins, and green peas. Cook for 5 more minutes.
  9. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Tips & Variations

  • Liver Tip: Soak liver in milk for 30 minutes before cooking to mellow its strong flavor.
  • Vegetable Swap: Add chickpeas or garbanzos for extra texture and protein.
  • Sweet Variation: Some versions add pineapple chunks for a sweeter profile.
  • Alternative Protein: Use chicken instead of pork for a lighter version.
  • Make Ahead: Like most stews, menudo tastes even better the next day as flavors meld.
