Chicken Inasal

Grilled Chicken 1 hour (plus marinating) Serves 4

4.5 (120 reviews)

A Bacolod Specialty

Chicken Inasal is a beloved Visayan dish, especially popular in Bacolod City. What makes it special is the marinade—a bold mix of calamansi, vinegar, lemongrass, garlic, and annatto oil—that infuses the chicken with bright, tangy, and smoky flavors. It's traditionally grilled over hot coals and basted with annatto-infused oil for color and flavor.

Ingredients

  • 4 chicken leg quarters or thighs
  • 1/4 cup calamansi juice (or lemon juice)
  • 1/4 cup cane vinegar
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, pounded and chopped
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 cup annatto (atsuete) oil, for basting
  • Optional: chili flakes for heat

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine calamansi juice, vinegar, garlic, lemongrass, sugar, salt, and pepper to make the marinade.
  2. Add chicken and marinate for at least 3 hours or overnight for best flavor.
  3. Prepare annatto oil by heating oil with annatto seeds, then straining.
  4. Preheat a grill or grill pan. Remove chicken from marinade and grill over medium heat.
  5. Baste with annatto oil occasionally while grilling until chicken is cooked through and slightly charred.
  6. Serve with garlic rice, atchara (pickled papaya), and a dipping sauce of calamansi, soy sauce, and chili.

Tips & Variations

  • Oven Option: You can roast the chicken at 375°F (190°C) for 45–50 minutes if grilling isn't possible.
  • For Extra Flavor: Reserve some marinade (uncooked) as a dipping sauce.
  • More Authentic: Use native vinegar (sukang Iloko or tuba vinegar) and fresh calamansi.

Nutrition Information
