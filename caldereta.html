Beef Caldereta

Main Course 2 hours Serves 6 Medium

4.7 (92 reviews)

Rich & Hearty Filipino Stew

Beef Caldereta is a classic Filipino dish known for its tender beef chunks stewed in a rich tomato-based sauce. It's a comforting meal, often served during fiestas and special gatherings.

This dish is made even more flavorful with liver spread, potatoes, carrots, bell peppers, and sometimes cheese. It's deeply savory with just the right hint of spice, making it a Filipino favorite.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef chuck, cut into cubes
  • 1/4 cup liver spread
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1/2 cup grated cheese (optional)
  • 1-2 pcs chili (optional)
  • 2 cups beef broth or water
  • 3 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pot. Sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.
  2. Add beef and cook until browned on all sides.
  3. Stir in tomato sauce, tomato paste, and liver spread. Mix well.
  4. Pour in broth. Bring to a boil then reduce heat to simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours or until beef is tender.
  5. Add potatoes and carrots. Cook until slightly tender.
  6. Add bell peppers, cheese, and chili (if using). Simmer for another 5–10 minutes.
  7. Season with salt and pepper. Serve hot with rice.

Tips & Variations

  • Make It Spicy: Add more chili for a spicy kick.
  • Liver Spread Sub: Use pâté if liver spread is unavailable.
  • Cheesy Touch: Melted cheese adds creaminess and depth of flavor.
  • Meat Option: Substitute with goat meat (kalderetang kambing) for a traditional twist.
  • Prep Tip: Marinate beef in soy sauce and calamansi juice for 30 minutes before cooking for extra flavor.
