Tiyula Itum

Black Beef Soup 2 hours Serves 6

4.6 (89 reviews)

A Smoky Tausug Classic

Tiyula Itum is a black soup made by the Tausug people of Mindanao. It’s made with beef (or goat), ginger, lemongrass, and spices—but what makes it unique is the addition of burnt coconut meat or powder, which gives the dish its distinctive black color and deep smoky flavor. This is often served during special occasions like weddings and feasts.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg beef (with bones preferred, like short ribs or shank)
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, sliced
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, pounded
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/4 cup ground toasted coconut (or burnt coconut meat, pounded)
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: green chili (siling haba)

Instructions

  1. In a pot, heat oil and sauté garlic, onion, and ginger until fragrant.
  2. Add beef and cook until lightly browned on all sides.
  3. Add lemongrass and pour in water. Bring to a boil, then simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours until beef is tender.
  4. Add ground toasted coconut and stir well. Simmer for another 10–15 minutes.
  5. Season with salt and pepper. Add chili if using.
  6. Serve hot with steamed rice. Best enjoyed with native vinegar or sinamak on the side.

Tips & Variations

  • To Make Toasted Coconut: Grate fresh coconut meat and toast it in a pan until blackened but not burnt. Then grind to a fine powder.
  • Meat Option: You can also use goat meat (kambing) for a more traditional version.
  • Flavor Depth: Let the soup rest for a few hours or overnight for a richer taste.

Nutrition Information

Serving Size: 1 cup | Calories: 350 | Protein: 30g | Fat: 24g | Carbs: 5g | Sodium: 500mg
