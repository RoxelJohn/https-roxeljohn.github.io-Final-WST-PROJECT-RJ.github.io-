Halo-Halo

Dessert 30 mins Serves 4 Easy

4.7 (150 reviews)

Philippines' Iconic Shaved Ice Dessert

Halo-Halo, which means "mix-mix" in Filipino, is the ultimate tropical dessert that perfectly combats the sweltering Philippine heat. This colorful layered dessert combines sweetened fruits, beans, jellies, and other delightful ingredients topped with shaved ice and evaporated milk.

The beauty of Halo-Halo lies in its customizable nature - each spoonful offers a different combination of flavors and textures, from the creamy leche flan to the crunchy pinipig rice crispies, all brought together by the sweet, milky ice.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups shaved ice
  • 1 cup evaporated milk
  • 1/2 cup sweetened banana (saba)
  • 1/2 cup sweetened jackfruit (langka)
  • 1/2 cup sweetened red beans
  • 1/2 cup sweetened white beans
  • 1/2 cup coconut strips (macapuno)
  • 1/2 cup sweetened yam (ube halaya)
  • 1/2 cup nata de coco (coconut jelly)
  • 1/2 cup kaong (sugar palm fruit)
  • 1/2 cup leche flan (caramel custard)
  • 1/4 cup pinipig (toasted rice crispies)
  • 2 scoops ube ice cream (purple yam flavor)
  • 1/4 cup sugar (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare all ingredients by cutting them into bite-sized pieces if needed.
  2. In a tall glass or bowl, layer the ingredients starting with the heaviest items at the bottom (beans, fruits, jellies).
  3. Add a layer of shaved ice, filling about 3/4 of the container.
  4. Drizzle evaporated milk over the ice (about 1/4 cup per serving).
  5. Add a scoop of ube halaya (yam jam) on top of the ice.
  6. Top with leche flan and a scoop of ube ice cream.
  7. Sprinkle pinipig (rice crispies) over the top for crunch.
  8. Serve immediately with a long spoon. Mix all ingredients together before eating.

Tips & Variations

  • For a quicker version, use ready-made halo-halo ingredient mixes available in Filipino stores.
  • Substitute fresh fruits like mango or pineapple for a different flavor profile.
  • Try different ice cream flavors - mango, coconut, or vanilla work well too.
  • Make it vegan by using coconut milk instead of evaporated milk and dairy-free ice cream.
  • For special occasions, serve in coconut shells for a tropical presentation.
