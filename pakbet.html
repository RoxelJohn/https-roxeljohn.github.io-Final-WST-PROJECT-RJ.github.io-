Pinakbet

Vegetable Dish 45 minutes Serves 4–6 Healthy

4.5 (101 reviews)

A Classic Ilocano Vegetable Medley

Pinakbet (or Pakbet) is a beloved Filipino dish from the north, particularly Ilocos, that combines a variety of local vegetables sautéed with shrimp paste (bagoong alamang or isda). This dish is savory, earthy, and full of umami — and a perfect way to enjoy seasonal produce.

Traditionally cooked without water and often in clay pots, pinakbet brings together bitter, sweet, and salty flavors in a rustic and nourishing way.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 kilo pork belly, sliced thin
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons shrimp paste (bagoong)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup squash, cubed
  • 1 cup eggplant, sliced
  • 1 cup bitter melon (ampalaya), sliced
  • 1 cup okra, trimmed
  • 1/2 cup string beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch lengths
  • 1 medium tomato, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a pan, render pork belly until browned and some fat has been released.
  2. Add garlic, onion, and tomato. Sauté until soft and fragrant.
  3. Stir in the shrimp paste and cook for another 2–3 minutes.
  4. Add water and bring to a boil.
  5. Add squash and cook for about 5 minutes until slightly tender.
  6. Add the rest of the vegetables: eggplant, bitter melon, okra, and string beans. Stir gently to combine.
  7. Cover and simmer until all vegetables are cooked but not mushy, about 10–12 minutes.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Tips & Variations

  • Ilocano Style: Use fermented fish paste (bagoong isda) for a more authentic northern taste.
  • No-Pork Option: Skip the pork and use mushrooms or tofu for a vegetarian version.
  • No-Water Method: Cook veggies in their own juices for a drier, more traditional pinakbet.
  • Sweet Touch: Add a bit of squash early to naturally sweeten the broth.
