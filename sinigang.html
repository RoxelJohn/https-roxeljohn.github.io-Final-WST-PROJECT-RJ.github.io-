Sinigang

Soup 1 hour 15 mins Serves 6 Easy

4.8 (95 reviews)

The Perfect Sour Soup

Sinigang is the ultimate Filipino comfort food - a sour soup that's both refreshing and hearty. The signature sourness comes from tamarind (or other souring agents like guava or calamansi), balanced with savory broth and fresh vegetables.

This version uses pork, but you can easily substitute with shrimp, fish, or beef. The sour broth stimulates the appetite and pairs perfectly with steamed rice.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork ribs or belly (cut into chunks)
  • 1 packet sinigang mix (or 1 cup tamarind pulp)
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach)
  • 1 eggplant (sliced)
  • 6 pieces okra
  • 2 tomatoes (quartered)
  • 1 onion (quartered)
  • 2 pieces gabi (taro root, peeled and quartered)
  • 2 pieces long green chili (siling haba)
  • 8 cups water
  • Fish sauce to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add pork and simmer for 30-40 minutes until tender.
  2. Add onions and tomatoes. Simmer for 3 minutes.
  3. Add gabi and simmer for 6-8 minutes until tender.
  4. Add sinigang mix or tamarind pulp. Stir well.
  5. Add eggplant and okra. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  6. Add kangkong and green chili. Simmer for 2 minutes.
  7. Season with fish sauce to taste. Serve hot.
