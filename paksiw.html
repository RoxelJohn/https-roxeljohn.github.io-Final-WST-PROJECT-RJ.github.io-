Paksiw na Isda

Main Course 30 minutes Serves 4 Easy

4.2 (67 reviews)

Tangy, Light, and Flavorful

Paksiw na Isda is a traditional Filipino dish that features fish simmered in vinegar, garlic, ginger, and vegetables. It’s a simple and healthy comfort food often enjoyed with steamed rice.

This dish has a sour and slightly salty taste, with a bit of heat depending on how much chili you add. It's a staple in coastal Filipino households, often using bangus (milkfish), galunggong (round scad), or tilapia.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb bangus (milkfish), cleaned and sliced
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 4 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 long green chili peppers
  • 1 small eggplant, sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a wide pan, layer the fish slices. Add ginger, garlic, onion, eggplant, and chili on top.
  2. Pour in vinegar and water. Do not stir.
  3. Bring to a boil uncovered. Let boil for 3–5 minutes to cook off the acidity of the vinegar.
  4. Cover the pan and lower the heat. Simmer for 15–20 minutes or until fish is cooked and flavors are well combined.
  5. Season with salt and pepper. Add oil if desired for extra richness.
  6. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Tips & Variations

  • No Stirring: Avoid stirring after adding vinegar to prevent the fish from breaking apart.
  • Other Fish Options: Try tilapia, galunggong, or tamban.
  • Veggie Boost: Add okra or ampalaya (bitter gourd) for variety.
  • Oil Finish: A bit of oil adds richness and balances acidity.
  • Storage: Paksiw actually tastes better the next day!
