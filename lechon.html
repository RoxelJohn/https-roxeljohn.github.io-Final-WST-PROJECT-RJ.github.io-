Lechon

Main Course 6-8 hours Serves 15-20 Advanced

4.6 (75 reviews)

The King of Filipino Festive Foods

Lechon, the whole roasted pig, is the centerpiece of Filipino celebrations. The crisp, golden-brown skin and tender, flavorful meat make it worth the effort of preparation.

This recipe provides instructions for a home oven version, though traditional lechon is cooked over charcoal on a spit. The secret to perfect lechon is in the seasoning and constant basting.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole suckling pig (10-15 lbs) or pork belly (5-7 lbs)
  • 1 head garlic (crushed)
  • 2 onions (quartered)
  • 10 stalks lemongrass (crushed)
  • 1 bunch spring onions
  • 5 bay leaves
  • 1/2 cup salt
  • 1/4 cup ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1/2 cup cooking oil (for basting)
  • 1 bottle of soda (7-up or Sprite)

Instructions

  1. Clean the pig thoroughly. Rub inside and out with salt and pepper.
  2. Combine garlic, onions, lemongrass, spring onions, and bay leaves. Stuff the cavity with this mixture.
  3. Sew or skewer the cavity closed. Rub the skin with soda to help crisp it.
  4. Place on a rack in a roasting pan. Roast at 325째F (160째C) for 4-6 hours (depending on size).
  5. Baste every 30 minutes with a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, and oil.
  6. Increase heat to 400째F (200째C) for the last 30 minutes to crisp the skin.
  7. Let rest for 30 minutes before carving. Serve with lechon sauce.
