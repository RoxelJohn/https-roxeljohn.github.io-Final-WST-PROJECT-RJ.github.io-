Kare-Kare

Main Course 3 hours Serves 6 Medium

4.2 (85 reviews)

Filipino Peanut Stew

Kare-Kare is a rich Filipino stew characterized by its thick peanut sauce and tender oxtail. This beloved dish is traditionally served during special occasions and family gatherings, featuring a unique combination of flavors from peanuts, annatto, and savory meat.

The magic of Kare-Kare lies in its velvety peanut sauce that perfectly complements the tender oxtail and fresh vegetables. It's always served with bagoong (shrimp paste) on the side, adding a salty contrast to the nutty flavors.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs oxtail (cut into segments)
  • 1 lb beef tripe (optional)
  • 1 cup smooth peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup toasted ground rice
  • 1 eggplant (sliced)
  • 1 bundle string beans (sitaw, cut into 2-inch pieces)
  • 2 bunches bok choy (pechay)
  • 1 banana heart (puso ng saging, sliced)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 6 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 tbsp annatto seeds (atsuete)
  • 6 cups beef broth
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce (patis)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Bagoong (shrimp paste) for serving

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, boil oxtail and tripe until very tender (about 2-3 hours). Skim off scum that rises to the surface. Reserve the broth.
  2. Soak annatto seeds in 1/4 cup warm water for 10 minutes, then strain to extract the color.
  3. In a separate pan, toast rice until golden brown then grind into powder using a food processor.
  4. In a large pot, saut√© garlic and onions until fragrant. Add the boiled meat and cook for 2 minutes.
  5. Pour in beef broth and bring to a boil. Add the annatto water for color.
  6. Stir in peanut butter and ground toasted rice. Simmer for 15 minutes until sauce thickens.
  7. Add fish sauce and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Blanch vegetables separately (eggplant, string beans, banana heart, bok choy) and arrange on a serving platter.
  9. Pour the peanut sauce with meat over the vegetables. Serve hot with steamed rice and bagoong on the side.

Tips & Variations

  • For a quicker version, use a pressure cooker to tenderize the oxtail (about 45 minutes).
  • Some versions use pork hocks or beef chunks instead of oxtail.
  • Add 1/2 cup coconut milk for a richer, creamier sauce (Kare-Kare sa Gata).
  • Toast your own peanuts and grind them for a more authentic peanut flavor.
  • Leftover Kare-Kare tastes even better the next day as the flavors meld together.
