Lumpiang Shanghai

Appetizer 1 hour Makes 25 pieces Medium

4.8 (200 reviews)

Filipino Crispy Spring Rolls

Lumpiang Shanghai is the Filipino version of crispy spring rolls, featuring a savory pork filling wrapped in thin egg roll wrappers. These golden-brown delights are a staple at Filipino parties and gatherings, always the first to disappear from the appetizer table.

What makes Lumpia special is its perfect crispiness that gives way to a flavorful, juicy filling. Served with sweet chili sauce or banana ketchup, these bite-sized rolls are impossible to resist. The name "Shanghai" reflects the Chinese influence on Filipino cuisine, though this version is distinctly Pinoy.

Ingredients

For the filling:

  • 1 lb ground pork
  • 1/2 lb shrimp, peeled and minced
  • 1 cup carrots, finely diced
  • 1 cup water chestnuts, finely diced
  • 1/2 cup green onions, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp salt

Other ingredients:

  • 25 pieces lumpia wrappers (spring roll wrappers)
  • 1 egg white (for sealing)
  • 2 cups cooking oil (for frying)
  • Sweet chili sauce or banana ketchup (for dipping)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine all filling ingredients (ground pork through salt). Mix thoroughly until well combined.
  2. Separate lumpia wrappers and keep covered with damp towel to prevent drying.
  3. Place about 1 tablespoon of filling near one corner of wrapper. Shape into a log about 3 inches long.
  4. Fold corner over filling, then fold sides inward. Roll tightly toward remaining corner.
  5. Brush end of wrapper with egg white to seal. Repeat with remaining wrappers and filling.
  6. Heat oil in deep pan to 350°F (175°C). Fry lumpia in batches until golden brown (about 3-4 minutes per batch).
  7. Drain on paper towels. Serve hot with dipping sauce.

Tips & Variations

  • For crispier lumpia, double-fry: Fry until lightly golden, drain, then fry again at slightly higher temperature.
  • Make ahead: Unfried lumpia can be frozen for up to 1 month. Fry directly from frozen, adding 1-2 minutes to cooking time.
  • Vegetarian option: Replace meat with tofu, mushrooms, and additional vegetables.
  • For party-size lumpia, cut rolls in half diagonally before serving.
  • Add 1/4 cup grated cheddar cheese to filling for extra richness.
